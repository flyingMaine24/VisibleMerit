# Proof of Work — Product Brief & Ideation Document

## 1. Core Concept

**Proof of Work** is a career translation tool for frontline workers who want to move into better opportunities but struggle to understand how their experience maps to corporate, operational, technical, customer success, product, leadership, or business roles.

The product helps users turn real-world work experience into:

- Transferable skills
- Better-fit career paths
- Resume bullets
- LinkedIn profile language
- Interview stories
- Promotion narratives
- Skills gap checklists
- Practical next steps

This is not coaching. This is a structured paid tool that generates useful career artifacts from guided user inputs.

## 3. The Problem

Frontline workers often face four major career barriers:

### 1. They do not know what roles are available.

Many workers only know the jobs they have directly seen. They may not know that their experience could translate into roles like:

- Operations Analyst
- Customer Success Manager
- Implementation Specialist
- Product Operations Associate
- Program Coordinator
- Training Specialist
- Workforce Coordinator
- Business Analyst
- Safety Coordinator
- Logistics Coordinator
- Product Manager, eventually
- Process Improvement Analyst
- Airport Operations Specialist
- Vendor Operations Manager

### 2. They undervalue their own experience.

They may describe high-value work with low-value language.

Example:

> “I helped with daily operations.”

But the actual value may be:

> “Coordinated daily operational workflows across multiple teams to maintain service continuity, resolve exceptions, and support on-time execution.”

### 3. They do not know how to translate frontline work into transferable skills.

Common frontline work often includes:

- Problem-solving
- Escalation management
- Customer communication
- Safety awareness
- Process execution
- Training
- Workflow improvement
- Cross-functional coordination
- Time-sensitive decision-making
- Systems usage
- Quality control
- Team leadership

But users often do not label these as professional skills.

### 4. They lack polished artifacts.

They may not have:

- Strong resume bullets
- A clear LinkedIn headline
- A compelling LinkedIn About section
- Interview stories
- A career narrative
- A list of target roles
- A transition plan

Proof of Work solves this by turning user inputs into usable career assets.

## What the Pack Includes

The Career Translation Pack should generate:

1. **Transferable Skills Report**
   - 5–10 skills based on user experience
   - Plain-English explanation of why each skill matters
   - Corporate language version of each skill

2. **Best-Fit Role Paths**
   - 5–7 roles the user may be qualified for or close to qualified for
   - Why the role fits
   - What gaps may exist
   - Suggested next step for each role

3. **Resume Summary**
   - A polished professional summary tailored to the target role or industry

4. **Resume Bullets**
   - 8–12 role-specific bullets
   - Metrics-based where possible
   - Written in business-friendly language

5. **LinkedIn Headline**
   - 3–5 headline options

6. **LinkedIn About Section**
   - One complete version
   - Optional shorter version

7. **Interview Stories**
   - 3 STAR-format stories
   - Each tied to common behavioral interview themes

8. **Career Narrative**
   - A short “tell me about yourself” answer
   - A longer career transition story

9. **Skills Gap Checklist**
   - Skills to build
   - Tools to learn
   - Certifications to consider
   - Experience to highlight

10. **30-Day Move-Up Plan**
   - Update resume
   - Update LinkedIn
   - Identify target roles
   - Reach out to contacts
   - Apply strategically
   - Practice interview stories

## 7. Core User Flow

## Step 1: User Chooses Their Current Work Type

Examples:

- Airport / Airline Operations
- Warehouse / Logistics
- Retail / Hospitality
- Customer Service / Call Center
- Field Service / Technician
- Healthcare Support
- Manufacturing / Production
- Food Service / Restaurant
- Other Frontline Work

## Step 2: User Chooses Their Goal

Examples:

- I want a better job but do not know what roles fit me
- I want to move into corporate
- I want to update my resume
- I want to prepare for interviews
- I want to move into customer success
- I want to move into operations leadership
- I want to move into product or tech
- I want to apply to school or an MBA program
- I want to prepare for a promotion

## Step 3: Guided Experience Intake

Questions should be plain, specific, and non-intimidating.

Example questions:

1. What is your current or most recent role?
2. What industry do you work in?
3. What does a normal day look like?
4. What problems do you solve most often?
5. What tools, systems, or equipment do you use?
6. Who do you communicate with daily?
7. What happens when something goes wrong?
8. Have you trained anyone?
9. Have you improved a process?
10. Have you helped reduce errors, delays, complaints, or rework?
11. Have you handled escalations or difficult customers?
12. Have you led shifts, projects, teams, or handoffs?
13. What are you proud of from this role?
14. What kind of job do you want next?
15. What kind of work do you want to avoid?
16. Are there industries you are interested in?
17. Do you have any metrics, even rough estimates?
18. What education, certifications, or tools do you already have?

## Step 4: AI Translation Engine

The tool translates the user's answers into:

- Professional skills
- Role matches
- Resume language
- Interview stories
- LinkedIn copy
- Skill gaps
- Action plan

## Step 5: Output Page

The user receives a clean report that can be:

- Viewed online
- Copied section by section
- Downloaded as PDF
- Downloaded as DOCX
- Downloaded as Markdown
- Saved to user account later

## 15. Risks and Mitigations

## Risk 1: Name confusion

**Problem:** Proof of Work may sound like blockchain, developer portfolios, or work verification.

**Mitigation:** Always pair it with a clear tagline:

> Real work deserves better words.

And a clear explainer:

> Career translation for frontline workers ready for better opportunities.

## Risk 2: Users may not pay upfront

**Problem:** Some users may need to see value first.

**Mitigation:** Offer a free mini-output:

- 3 transferable skills
- 2 possible roles
- 1 sample resume bullet

Then charge for full pack.

## Risk 3: Generic AI output

**Problem:** If outputs sound like generic ChatGPT, users will not value it.

**Mitigation:** Use structured prompts, examples, industry-specific language, and before/after transformations.

## Risk 4: Too broad an audience

**Problem:** “Frontline workers” is broad.

**Mitigation:** Start with one or two verticals.

Recommended starting verticals:

1. Airport / airline / aviation operations
2. Customer support / customer service
3. Warehouse / logistics

Given Jermaine's background, aviation and operations should be the first credibility wedge.

## Risk 5: Users may need more support than a tool provides

**Problem:** Some users may want coaching.

**Mitigation:** Keep the product artifact-based. Offer clear examples, templates, and next steps, but avoid promising job placement or personal coaching.

## 18. Product Principles

1. **Start with the user’s real work.**
2. **Use plain questions, not career jargon.**
3. **Translate, do not exaggerate.**
4. **Show the user what roles may fit.**
5. **Give them artifacts they can immediately use.**
6. **Do not promise job placement.**
7. **Do not pretend every user is qualified for every role.**
8. **Be honest about gaps.**
9. **Make outputs easy to copy, download, and edit.**
10. **Keep the MVP narrow.**
